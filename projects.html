4D Gaussian World Models for Dynamic Scene Understanding
Active

We extend 3D Gaussian splatting to the temporal domain to build compact, editable 4D representations of dynamic scenes. The resulting world models support downstream tasks including object tracking, novel-view synthesis under motion, and physical scene simulation with minimal supervision.

3D Gaussian Splatting Neural Rendering Dynamic Scenes CVPR 2026 target
NSF IIS-2341022 Google Research Award
Sofia Rodriguez · Elena Baranova · Priya Sharma
Repo

Embodied Vision-Language Agents for Open-World Navigation
Active

We develop language-grounded embodied agents capable of navigating complex indoor and outdoor environments using natural language instructions. Our agents leverage vision-language models for zero-shot affordance prediction and build persistent spatial memory to reason about previously-seen objects.

Embodied AI VLMs Navigation Spatial Reasoning
NSF RI-2246801 DARPA SAIL-ON
Marcus Kim · Sofia Rodriguez · Daniel Park
Repo
03
🎬
Long-Horizon Temporal Understanding of Instructional Video
Active

Reasoning about hour-long instructional videos requires understanding causal chains, tracking state changes, and predicting procedural next steps. We build hierarchical temporal models trained with weak procedural supervision from online tutorial data at massive scale.

Video Understanding Temporal Reasoning Procedural AI
Microsoft Research
Marcus Kim · Yuki Tanaka
Repo
04
🔬
Geometric Depth and Tissue Segmentation for Surgical AI
Active

Real-time 3D understanding of surgical scenes can dramatically improve robot-assisted procedures. We develop self-supervised monocular depth estimation and tissue-aware semantic segmentation tailored to the challenging photometric conditions of endoscopic imaging.

Medical Vision Depth Estimation Surgical AI
NIH R01-EB033456
Yuki Tanaka · Priya Sharma · James Chen
Repo
05
🧠
Open-Vocabulary 3D Object Understanding at Scale
Active

We distill knowledge from 2D vision-language foundation models into 3D scene representations, enabling open-vocabulary 3D object grounding, part segmentation, and attribute prediction without 3D-specific annotation.

Vision-Language 3D Understanding Open-Vocabulary
Samsung Research
Daniel Park · James Chen · Alex Morgan
Repo
06
✨
Cognitive Priors for Few-Shot Visual Scene Completion
Starting

Humans fill in occluded regions of scenes effortlessly using learned cognitive priors. We investigate how similar priors can be instilled in neural networks to enable robust scene completion and object permanence reasoning from just a handful of observations.

Scene Completion Object Permanence Few-Shot Learning
NSF CAREER Award
Elena Baranova · Alex Morgan
